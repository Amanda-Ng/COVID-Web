What exactly is COVID-19?

Coronavirus refers to the family of viruses that can cause symptoms including, but not limted to, severe acute respiratory syndrome (MERS), the common cold, and Middle East respiratory syndrome (MERS).


In 2019, a new strain of coronavirus was determined to be the cause of an outbreak originating in China. Known as severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-COV-2), this disease is more commonly called COVID-19. In March of 2020, the World Heath Organization (WHO) officialy declared the COVID-19 outbreak a pandemic.


COVID-19 is an infectious disease that causes a virus, and those with the virus will experience mild to moderate respiratory illness, recovering without any special treatments. Individuals more susceptible to COVID-19 include older people and those with underlying medical conditions including diabetes, chronic respiratory disease, and asthma.



Symptoms

Symptoms of COVID-19 may include the following:

fever

loss of taste and/or smell

tiredness

sore throat

aches and pains

headache
